Do's:

1. Do review your privacy settings: Make sure that your personal information and casual photos are       only accessible to your close friends and family.

2. Do think before you post: Remember that nothing is ever really deleted from the internet and that your future employer might look at what you have posted.

3. Do use social media to your advantage: Share your academic and professional achievements and articles related to your field of study on social media sites such as LinkedIn.

4. Do respect other people's privacy: Always ask for permission before posting photos or tagging your friends, classmates, or professors.

5. Do fact-check before you share: Before you share any news or information, always verify it to ensure that it is not false.

Don'ts:

1. Don't over-share personal details: It is not advisable to share personal details such as location, daily routine, phone number, and student ID number, as it compromises personal safety.

2. Don't engage in cyberbullying and arguments: It is not advisable to participate in arguments and cyberbullying, and it is always best to be civil in social media interactions.

3. Don't vent about professors and college: It is not advisable to vent about professors and college in social media, as it reflects poorly on one’s professionalism.

4. Don't use inappropriate language and share explicit content: It is not advisable to share explicit content and inappropriate language, especially when one is looking for jobs, as most employers check social media profiles.

5. Don't accept friend requests from strangers: It is not advisable to accept friend requests from strangers, especially in social media sites like Facebook, which is for personal relationships.
