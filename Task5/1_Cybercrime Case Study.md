## ***Case Study: The 'Urgent Fee Payment' Scam via UPI***

## **What is the crime:** 
The crime here is 'UPI Scam,' where scammers induce people to make unauthorized transactions using digital payment apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.

## **How it occurs (Step-by-Step):**

   1. A college student receives an urgent text message or call from someone impersonating a person from the accounts department of their college.

   2. The caller tells the student that their semester registration is on hold because they have to pay a fee of Rs 5,000, which they have not paid yet.

   3. The student then receives a payment link or a QR code via WhatsApp.

   4. In a state of emergency, the student clicks on this link, which opens their UPI app directly.

   5. The student is then asked to enter their UPI PIN code. The student enters their PIN code unknowingly, thinking they are making a legitimate payment to their college. However, this money
      is immediately deducted and transferred to the scammer's bank account.

## **Who is usually targeted:** 
Most victims of this type of scam are college students or young adults because they use digital financial platforms for transactions. Scammers use social engineering techniques to trick their victims by creating a false sense of urgency or fear of being punished (e.g., for being late to submit an assignment) to avoid critical thinking.

## **What are the consequences:** 
The first effect of this type of scam is a direct financial loss, which can be catastrophic for a college student on a tight budget. Furthermore, it can lead to psychological stress and a loss of trust in digital financial systems. It is also difficult to recover money because the victim has willingly authorized a transaction by inserting their PIN.
