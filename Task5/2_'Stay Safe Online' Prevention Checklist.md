## ***Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for Students***

1. Remember the Golden Rule of UPI: Your UPI PIN is required only to send money from your account and not to receive money. If an app asks you to enter your PIN to 'Claim a Refund' or 'Receive a Prize,' it is a scam.

2. Verify Recipient Details: Always verify the recipient’s name and VPA on your screen before making any payment using UPI, especially in urgent cases.

3. Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Use complex passwords for all your online accounts. Avoid using easily guessable information like your college id, date of birth, or phone numbers.

4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on your email accounts, social media accounts, and banking apps to add an essential extra layer of security against cybercrime.

5. Beware of Phishing Links: Avoid clicking on suspicious links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email, even if they are from reputed organizations or your friends.

6. Limit Personal Information on Social Media: It is not advisable to share too much personal information, like your location, routine, or financial status, which can be used by scammers to build a profile about you.

7. Avoid Financial Transactions on Public Wi-Fi: It is not advisable to make financial transactions while using public free Wi-Fi, like in coffee shops, libraries, or in the airport, as it is not a secure network.

8. Report Cybercrimes Immediately: If you suspect yourself to be a victim of cybercrime, like online fraud, cyberbullying, or identity theft, report it immediately to the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal cybercrime.gov.in or call the National Cyber Crime Helpline 1930.
